WHAT is Bullying?

Definition

Bullying is a known social phenomenon. The word bullying has English origins, mob - rabble. It used to describe the people mass or a herd. Word is negatively charged with respect to anger and rebellion. (Olweus, 1992, p. 17). One of the most commonly used definitions of bullying is, violence or harassment, of a physical or mental character, which is extending over time, and is directed toward a victim and carried out by an individual or a group. (Storfjord, 1997, p. 89).


Different types of bullying

There are different characteristics of bullying. Research on bullying has identified three different types of bullying:

  • Mental bullying - offensive statements, unpleasant messages or intimidation, ridicule
  • Social bullying - exclusion, untrue and/or malicious rumors competent
  • Physical bullying - punches, kicks, lugging, vandalism on clothing or belongings
  • Cyber bullying - this is latest form of bullying that came along with technological developments. It involves the use of information and communication technology (usually the Internet) to cantilever of individuals. (Hanewald, 2013, p. 5).

For something to be called bullying, there must be a certain imbalance in the power relationship. The bullies are much stronger physically and mentally than the one being bullied, who cannot defend themselves and are often helpeless against the person or persons who bully. (Utdanningsdirektoratet, 2005, p.11). Causes of bullying can be social status, conflicts of interests, appearance or other personal characteristics.


Consequences of Bullying

Bullying has very negative consequences for both the bully and the victim. It is so that the word bullying is associated primarily with bullying that takes place in schools, unfortunately that is not the only place where this kind of abuse is happening. Research says that bullying also takes place in kindergartens and on workplaces, and has a great impact on people's lives.
